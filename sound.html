Sound Structure & Writing System

1. Vowels

1.1 Vowel Letters & IPA

Čeluria has five basic vowel letters, exactly like Spanish:

Letter IPA Category
A /a/ Strong
E /e/ Strong
O /o/ Strong
I /i/ Weak
U /u/ Weak

  5. SC → /ʃ/

3. Syllable Structure: The Detailed Rules

3.1

Identify recognized clusters first. If you see such a cluster at the beginning of what could be a new syllable, treat it as a single unit (onset).

3.2

Otherwise, if multiple consonants appear and do not form a recognized cluster, place all but the last consonant(s) as the coda of the preceding syllable, and the last consonant becomes the onset of the next syllable.

3.3

Within each syllable: after the onset (which may be a single consonant or a cluster), all contiguous vowels belong to the nucleus (forming single vowels, diphthongs, or triphthongs) until you reach the next consonant (or the word ends).

3.4 Stress Rules

Default stress on the second-to-last syllable; use an acute accent if you want to override.

4. Examples

4.1 kaino

  • Letters: k-a-i-n-o
  • Syllable #1: k (onset) + “ai” (diphthong) → /kaj/
  • No next consonant until “n,” so we gather “ai.”
  • Next consonant = “n,” becomes onset of Syllable #2
  • Then vowel “o” is included in Syllable #2
  • Syllables: [kai]-[no] = /kaj.no/
  • Penultimate stress for a 2-syllable word → /ˈkaj.no/

4.2 maiso

  • Letters: m-a-i-s-o
  • Syllable #1: m + “ai” = /maj/
  • Next consonant = “s,” onset of next syllable
  • Syllable #2: [s] + “o” → /so/
  • [mai]-[so] = /maj.so/ → /ˈmaj.so/

4.3 gandro

  • Letters: g-a-n-d-r-o
  • Consecutive consonants: “n, d, r.” Each is single-letter (none merges like Š or Č)
  • Rule: all but last consonant in a sequence → coda; last consonant → next onset
  • Syllable #1 onset: g + “a.” Then “n, d, r” appear consecutively.
  • n, d go to coda → [g-a-n-d], r becomes onset of Syllable #2 with “o” → [r-o]
  • Syllables: [gand]-[ro] = /gand.ʁo/ → /ˈgand.ʁo/

4.4 šaño

  • Letters: š-a-ñ-o
  • Š = /ʃ/, Ñ = /ɲ/ are single consonants
  • Syllable #1: [š] + “a” = /ʃa/
  • Syllable #2: [ñ] + “o” = /ɲo/
  • [ša]-[ño] = /ˈʃa.ɲo/

4.5 čeuno

  • Letters: č-e-u-n-o
  • Č = /tʃ/ is a single consonant
  • Syllable #1: [č] + “eu” → /tʃew/
  • Next consonant = “n,” onset of Syllable #2 with “o” → [n-o]
  • [čeu]-[no] = /ˈtʃew.no/
